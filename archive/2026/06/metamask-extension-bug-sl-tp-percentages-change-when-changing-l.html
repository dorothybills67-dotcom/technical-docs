Metamask Extension Bug Sl Tp Percentages Change When Changing L

Symptom

  • Users of the Metamask extension on the Ethereum (ETH) ecosystem may observe that their Stop-Loss (SL) and Take-Profit (TP) percentages unexpectedly change when they adjust the leverage of their positions.

Technical Root Cause

  • The error occurs due to a bug in the Metamask extension's handling of leverage adjustments, which inadvertently alters the SL and TP percentages set by the user. This discrepancy arises from a mismatch in the internal calculations of the extension when leverage is modified.

Manual Overrides

  • To temporarily resolve the issue, users can attempt the following manual overrides:
  • Manually recalculate and reset the SL and TP percentages after each leverage change.
  • Utilize an external calculator to determine the correct SL and TP percentages based on the new leverage.
  • Apply a custom script to automatically adjust the SL and TP percentages in response to leverage changes, such as:
#!/bin/bash
LEVERAGE=$1
SL_PERCENTAGE=$2
TP_PERCENTAGE=$3
# Calculate and apply new SL and TP percentages based on the provided leverage
NEW_SL_PERCENTAGE=$(echo "scale=2; $SL_PERCENTAGE * $LEVERAGE" | bc)
NEW_TP_PERCENTAGE=$(echo "scale=2; $TP_PERCENTAGE * $LEVERAGE" | bc)
echo "New SL Percentage: $NEW_SL_PERCENTAGE"
echo "New TP Percentage: $NEW_TP_PERCENTAGE"

Consider using a dedicated terminal for more complex adjustments.

Prerequisites

  • Metamask extension installed and configured.
  • Basic understanding of leverage and SL/TP percentages in trading.
  • Familiarity with using bash scripts or similar automation tools.
