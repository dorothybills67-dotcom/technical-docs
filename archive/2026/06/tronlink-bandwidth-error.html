Symptom

  • Users encounter a "TronLink bandwidth error" when attempting to perform transactions or interact with smart contracts on the TRON network using TronLink, a popular browser extension wallet.

Technical Root Cause

  • The error occurs due to insufficient bandwidth or energy on the user's TRON account, which is required to process transactions and execute smart contracts on the network. This can be caused by a lack of TRX (TRON's native cryptocurrency) in the account or insufficient bandwidth allocation.

Manual Overrides

  • Increase the account's TRX balance to acquire more bandwidth.
  • Adjust the bandwidth allocation settings in TronLink to prioritize specific transactions or contracts.
  • Utilize the following JSON-RPC command to check the account's current bandwidth:
{
  "jsonrpc": "2.0",
  "method": "tron_getAccount",
  "params": [
    "your_account_address"
  ],
  "id": 1
}

Consider utilizing an external console for more complex debugging and customization.

Prerequisites

  • A basic understanding of the TRON network and its resources (TRX, bandwidth, and energy).
  • The TronLink browser extension installed and configured.
  • A TRON account with a sufficient TRX balance to perform transactions.
